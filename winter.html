Carrot

The carrot (Daucus carota subsp. sativus) is a root vegetable, typically orange in color, though purple, black, red, white, and yellow cultivars exist,all of which are domesticated forms of the wild carrot, Daucus carota, native to Europe and Southwestern Asia. The plant probably originated in Persia and was originally cultivated for its leaves and seeds. The most commonly eaten part of the plant is the taproot, although the stems and leaves are also eaten. The domestic carrot has been selectively bred for its enlarged, more palatable, less woody-textured taproot. The carrot is a biennial plant in the umbellifer family, Apiaceae. At first, it grows a rosette of leaves while building up the enlarged taproot. Fast-growing cultivars mature within three months (90 days) of sowing the seed, while slower-maturing cultivars need a month longer (120 days). The roots contain high quantities of alpha- and beta-carotene, and are a good source of vitamin A, vitamin K, and vitamin B6.

Types of Carrot
  • Chantenay carrots
  • Danvers carrots
  • Imperator carrots
  • Nantes carrots

Cabbage

Cabbage, comprising several cultivars of Brassica oleracea, is a leafy green, red (purple), or white (pale green) biennial plant grown as an annual vegetable crop for its dense-leaved heads. It is descended from the wild cabbage (B. oleracea var. oleracea), and belongs to the "cole crops" or brassicas, meaning it is closely related to broccoli and cauliflower (var. botrytis); Brussels sprouts (var. gemmifera); and Savoy cabbage (var. sabauda). A cabbage generally weighs between 500 and 1,000 grams (1 and 2 lb). Smooth-leafed, firm-headed green cabbages are the most common, with smooth-leafed purple cabbages and crinkle-leafed savoy cabbages of both colours being rarer. Under conditions of long sunny days, such as those found at high northern latitudes in summer, cabbages can grow quite large. As of 2012, the heaviest cabbage was 62.71 kilograms (138 lb 4 oz). Cabbage heads are generally picked during the first year of the plant's life cycle, but plants intended for seed are allowed to grow a second year and must be kept separate from other cole crops to prevent cross-pollination. Cabbage is prone to several nutrient deficiencies, as well as to multiple pests, and bacterial and fungal diseases.

onion

Onion is a vegetable whose bulb is used as a vegetable. Onion cultivation is maximum in Maharashtra in India. Here onion is harvested twice a year - one in November and the other around the month of May. Onion is exported from India to many countries, such as Nepal, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, etc. Onion crop is ready at different time in places like Karnataka, Gujarat, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, Bihar, West Bengal Madhya Pradesh.In the world, onions are grown in an area of ​​1,789 thousand hectares, from which 25,387 thousand m. tons are produced. It is grown extensively in states like Maharashtra, Orissa, Karnataka, Uttar Pradesh, Tamil Nadu and Gujarat. It is a bulbous vegetable, whose tubers are used as a vegetable. The tuber is pungent. This pungency is due to a volatile oil called allyl propyl disulphide.Onion is used to prepare vegetables, spices, salads and pickles. Iron, calcium, and vitamin 'C' are found in the tuber. Tuber is pungent, sharp, forceful, aphrodisiac, flavourful, appetizing and blood-enhancing in women. spinach

Spinach (Botanical name: Spinacia oleracea) is a flowering plant of the Amaranthaceae family, whose leaves and stems are eaten as a vegetable. Mineral salts and vitamins are sufficient in spinach, but calcium is not available due to the presence of oxalic acid. It is native to Iran and its surrounding region. There are pre-Christian records in China, from which it is known that spinach went to China from Nepal. It reached Europe via Africa in the 12th century.Spinach can be grown easily in many types of soils, but it grows best on sandy, loamy, silty loams and loamy soils. It can tolerate acidity more. It requires 6.0 to 7.0 pH for adequate intelligence. Good drainage is necessary, otherwise the leaves get diseased.The main time for sowing of spinach seeds is after the rains and the sowing continues till November. Small scale sowing can be done even in the rainy season in raised lands. The seeds are sown in the field at a distance of 6 inches to 9 inches, at a depth of 10 cm and at a seed-to-seed distance. If the sowing is more dense then there is a fear of disease in the leaves. .

Types of spinach
  • Palco Spinach
  • Carmel Spinach
  • Acadia Spinach
  • Emperor Spinach

Green Pea

Pea is a flowering dicot plant. Knots are found in its root. Some leaflets next to its compound leaves turn into tendrils. It is a herbaceous plant whose stem is hollow. Its leaves are composed of seeds. Its flowers are full and butterfly shaped. Its pods are long, flat and with many seeds. The weight of one pea seed is 0.1 to 0.36 grams.Its center is South Asia. Production is carried out at medium heat. It is a one year old plant.In earlier times, green peas were eaten only when the season came, but now due to the increasing technology, the seeds of green peas are removed and frozen by vacuum, so that green peas can be found throughout the year, and in addition It is also used after drying the grains of green peas, after drying the green peas are broken into two parts and it is given the form of peas.

  • sugar snap peas
